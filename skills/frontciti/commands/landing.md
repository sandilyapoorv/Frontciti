# /frontciti landing

## Purpose

Design or improve landing pages, homepages, feature pages, pricing pages, marketing pages, and portfolios.

## Use When

Use when the goal is persuasion, explanation, trust, signup, sales, demo requests, or brand presentation.

## Inputs To Inspect

Product context, audience, current page, conversion goal, competitors, assets, proof points, pricing, FAQs, analytics clues, and existing brand rules.

## Workflow

1. Identify audience and desired action.
2. Define the page thesis.
3. Make the hero express that thesis.
4. Order sections around user objections and decision flow.
5. Use product proof, specific copy, and realistic visuals.
6. Place CTAs deliberately.
7. Polish responsive reading rhythm, hierarchy, imagery, and motion.

## Output Expectations

Use only sections that earn their place: hero, problem, product explanation, how it works, proof, features, use cases, social proof, pricing, FAQ, and final CTA.

## Anti-Patterns

- Generic centered gradient hero.
- "All-in-one platform" copy without specifics.
- Three-card feature rows with equal weight and no proof.
- Fake screenshots unrelated to the actual app.
- Abstract icons replacing evidence.

## Acceptance Checklist

- Above the fold says who it is for, what it does, why it matters, and what to do next.
- Sections flow logically.
- Copy is specific.
- CTA is visible and repeated appropriately.
- Mobile layout works.
- Visual style does not feel generic.
