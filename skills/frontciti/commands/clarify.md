# /frontciti clarify

## Purpose

Improve UX copy, labels, microcopy, errors, empty states, button text, and information clarity.

## Use When

Use when users may not understand what something means, what action does, what went wrong, or what to do next.

## Inputs To Inspect

Visible strings, form labels, helper text, errors, empty states, CTA labels, navigation, tooltips, success messages, and localization constraints.

## Workflow

1. Identify the user's mental model and task.
2. Replace system-centric wording with user-recognizable terms.
3. Use active verbs for actions.
4. Keep labels stable across action, loading, success, and error states.
5. Rewrite failures with cause and recovery.
6. Remove filler, hype, and unclear metaphors.

## Output Expectations

Copy should be plain, specific, consistent, and helpful without sounding generic or over-written.

## Anti-Patterns

- "Submit" when the action is specific.
- Vague errors like "Something went wrong."
- Placeholder-only labels.
- Sales cliches in product UI.
- Cute copy that obscures meaning.

## Acceptance Checklist

- Primary actions say what happens.
- Errors explain recovery.
- Empty states guide action.
- Terminology is consistent.
