# UX Copy

Words are interface material.

## Principles

- Write from the user's side of the screen.
- Name things by what users recognize, not implementation details.
- Use active voice.
- Keep action labels stable across action, loading, success, and error.
- Prefer specific plain language over cleverness.
- Match tone to register and risk.

## Actions

Buttons should say what happens:

- "Save changes" not "Submit."
- "Invite client" not "Continue" when the action is inviting.
- "Publish" should lead to "Published" or "Publication failed."

Use one label per CTA intent across a page.

## Errors

Error formula:

1. What happened.
2. Why, if known.
3. How to recover.

Avoid apologetic or vague errors. "Connection failed. Try again." is better than "Oops, something went wrong."

## Empty States

Empty states should:

- Explain why the space is empty.
- Tell the user what to do next.
- Offer an action when appropriate.
- Avoid cheerful filler when the state is stressful.

## Avoid

- "Seamless", "unlock", "elevate", "next-gen", "transform", and other filler claims.
- Placeholder names like "John Doe" and "Acme Corp."
- Fake precision without data.
- Overly poetic labels that obscure meaning.
- Title Case everywhere unless the brand requires it.
